# Meadow Checkermallow
![Meadow Checkermallow](../images/meadow-checkermallow.jpg)
*Photo: [Amy Bartow, NRCS Corvallis PMC](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Sidalcea_campestris.jpg) | Public Domain*

## Basic information
- **Scientific name:** Sidalcea campestris
- **Plant type:** Herbaceous Perennial
- **USDA zones:** 5-9
- **Native region:** Willamette Valley, Oregon (endemic)

## Growth characteristics
- **Mature height:** 2-5 feet (flower stalk; foliage much shorter)
- **Mature spread:** 8-24 inches
- **Growth rate:** Fast
- **Lifespan:** Long-lived perennial

## Growing conditions
- **Sun requirements:** Full Sun/Part Shade
- **Water needs:** Low-Medium (drought tolerant once established)
- **Soil type:** Well-drained; tolerates heavy clay that dries in summer
- **Soil pH:** 6.0-7.5

## Seasonal interest
- **Bloom time:** May-August
- **Bloom color:** White to blush pink
- **Fall color:** None significant
- **Winter interest:** Basal rosette of leaves remains

## Wildlife value
- **Attracts:** Native bees, butterflies, hummingbirds, beneficial insects
- **Host plant for:** Gray hairstreak, checkered skipper, West Coast lady, painted lady, American lady butterflies; Fender's blue butterfly (endangered)
- **Provides:** High-quality nectar and pollen; primary pollen source for native bee Diadasia nigrifrons

- **Location/bed:**

## Care & maintenance
- **Pruning needs:** Deadhead spent flowers to promote bloom; cut back to basal rosette in autumn
- **Fertilizer:** None needed
- **Mulch:** Light mulch; avoid overly wet conditions
- **Special care:** Protect from deer; ensure good air circulation to prevent leaf-spot or rust

## Notes
- **Design notes:** Hollyhock-like flowers on tall stems; excellent cut flower; important for pollinator habitat; makes a beautiful meadow planting
- **Observations:**
- **Challenges:** Deer browse the foliage; species of conservation concern in Oregon (largely extinct in wild)
