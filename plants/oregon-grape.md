# Tall Oregon Grape
![Tall Oregon Grape](../images/oregon-grape.jpg)
*Photo: [Didier Descouens](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:(MHNT)_Berberis_aquifolium_-_Flowers.jpg) | CC BY-SA 4.0*

## Basic information
- **Scientific name:** Mahonia aquifolium (syn. Berberis aquifolium)
- **Plant type:** Evergreen Shrub
- **USDA zones:** 5-9
- **Native region:** Pacific Northwest; British Columbia to northern California

## Growth characteristics
- **Mature height:** 3-6 feet
- **Mature spread:** 3-5 feet
- **Growth rate:** Medium
- **Lifespan:** Long-lived shrub
- **Roots:**

## Growing conditions
- **Sun requirements:** Part Shade/Full Shade (tolerates sun with moisture)
- **Water needs:** Low-Medium (drought tolerant once established)
- **Soil type:** Adaptable; tolerates poor, rocky soils
- **Soil pH:** 5.5-7.0
- **Native habitat:** Open to dense forests, woodland edges, rocky slopes

## Seasonal interest
- **Bloom time:** March-May
- **Bloom color:** Bright yellow, fragrant clusters
- **Fall color:** Evergreen; leaves may turn bronze/purple in winter
- **Winter interest:** Evergreen holly-like foliage; blue berries persist

## Wildlife value
- **Attracts:** Native bees, hummingbirds, birds
- **Host plant for:** Various butterfly and moth species
- **Provides:** Early nectar source; berries for birds; year-round cover

## Care & maintenance
- **Pruning needs:** Can prune after flowering to shape; remove old leggy stems at base
- **Fertilizer:** Generally not needed
- **Mulch:** 2-3 inches organic mulch
- **Special care:** Very low maintenance; Oregon state flower

## Notes
- **Design notes:** Excellent year-round structure; holly-like leaves without the sharp spines; bright yellow spring flowers followed by blue grape-like berries; tart berries edible for jams
- **Observations:**
- **Challenges:** Can spread by suckers; leaves may scorch in hot afternoon sun
