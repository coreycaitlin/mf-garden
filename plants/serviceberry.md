# Western Serviceberry

## Basic information
- **Scientific name:** Amelanchier alnifolia
- **Plant type:** Deciduous Shrub/Small Tree
- **USDA zones:** 2-7
- **Native region:** Western North America from Alaska to California, east to the Great Plains

## Growth characteristics
- **Mature height:** 6-15 feet (occasionally to 20 feet)
- **Mature spread:** 6-12 feet
- **Growth rate:** Medium
- **Lifespan:** Long-lived (50+ years)
- **Roots:**

## Growing conditions
- **Sun requirements:** Full Sun/Part Shade
- **Water needs:** Low-Medium (drought tolerant once established)
- **Soil type:** Adaptable; prefers well-drained
- **Soil pH:** 5.5-7.5 (adaptable)
- **Native habitat:**

## Seasonal interest
- **Bloom time:** April-May
- **Bloom color:** White, showy clusters
- **Fall color:** Yellow to red-orange
- **Winter interest:** Attractive multi-stemmed form; gray bark

## Wildlife value
- **Attracts:** Bees, birds, butterflies
- **Host plant for:** Various butterfly and moth species including swallowtails
- **Provides:** Highly valuable berries for birds and mammals; nesting sites

## Care & maintenance
- **Pruning needs:** Minimal; remove suckers if single-stem form desired
- **Fertilizer:** Generally not needed
- **Mulch:** 2-3 inches of organic mulch
- **Special care:** Very low maintenance once established

## Notes
- **Design notes:** Also called Saskatoon; excellent four-season interest with spring flowers, summer berries, fall color; edible berries great for pies and jams
- **Observations:**
- **Challenges:** May sucker; birds will compete for berries
