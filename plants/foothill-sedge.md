# Foothill Sedge
![Foothill Sedge](../images/foothill-sedge.jpg)
*Photo: [Daderot](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Carex_tumulicola_-_VanDusen_Botanical_Garden_-_Vancouver,_BC_-_DSC06771.jpg) | CC0*

## Basic information
- **Scientific name:** Carex tumulicola
- **Plant type:** Evergreen Sedge
- **USDA zones:** 7-10
- **Native region:** Pacific Northwest, west of the Cascades from northern California to British Columbia

## Growth characteristics
- **Mature height:** 18-24 inches
- **Mature spread:** 12-18 inches (spreads slowly by rhizomes)
- **Growth rate:** Medium
- **Lifespan:** Long-lived perennial

## Growing conditions
- **Sun requirements:** Part Shade/Part Sun (full sun along coast)
- **Water needs:** Low-Medium (tolerates drought once established)
- **Soil type:** Adaptable; tolerates various soils
- **Soil pH:** 5.5-7.5

## Seasonal interest
- **Bloom time:** April-June
- **Bloom color:** Cream-colored flower spikes
- **Fall color:** Evergreen
- **Winter interest:** Evergreen foliage persists

## Wildlife value
- **Attracts:** Songbirds, beneficial insects
- **Host plant for:** Native moths and butterflies
- **Provides:** Seeds for birds; nesting material and refugia for arthropods

## Care & maintenance
- **Pruning needs:** Comb out dead foliage in spring; can cut back if needed
- **Fertilizer:** None needed
- **Mulch:** Optional
- **Special care:** One of the few upland prairie sedges; not a wetland plant; very versatile and easy to grow

## Notes
- **Design notes:** Popular native grass alternative for lawns and groundcover; fine texture looks more refined in shade; excellent for erosion control, masses under trees, or meadow plantings
- **Observations:**
- **Challenges:** Foliage can become rough in full sun; prefers some shade inland
