# Heath Aster

## Basic information
- **Scientific name:** Symphyotrichum ericoides (syn. Aster ericoides)
- **Plant type:** Perennial
- **USDA zones:** 3-9
- **Native region:** North America, widespread from British Columbia to Maine and south

## Growth characteristics
- **Mature height:** 12-36 inches
- **Mature spread:** 12-24 inches
- **Growth rate:** Fast
- **Lifespan:** Short-lived perennial (3-5 years, but self-seeds)
- **Roots:**

## Growing conditions
- **Sun requirements:** Full Sun/Part Sun
- **Water needs:** Low-Medium (drought tolerant once established)
- **Soil type:** Adaptable; tolerates poor, dry, rocky soils
- **Soil pH:** 5.5-7.5
- **Native habitat:**

## Seasonal interest
- **Bloom time:** August-October (late season bloomer)
- **Bloom color:** White with yellow centers
- **Fall color:** Foliage may bronze
- **Winter interest:** Seed heads persist; winter structure

## Wildlife value
- **Attracts:** Bees, butterflies, beneficial insects
- **Host plant for:** Pearl Crescent and Silvery Checkerspot butterflies
- **Provides:** Late-season nectar (critical for pollinators), seeds for birds

- **Location/bed:**

## Care & maintenance
- **Pruning needs:** Cut back to 6 inches in late spring for bushier growth; cut to ground in late winter
- **Fertilizer:** None needed; thrives in poor soil
- **Mulch:** Light mulch or none
- **Special care:** Can be pinched back in early summer to reduce height and increase blooms

## Notes
- **Design notes:** Clouds of tiny white flowers create excellent texture; pairs well with fall grasses; valuable late-season pollinator plant
- **Observations:**
- **Challenges:** Can spread aggressively via rhizomes and self-seeding; may need dividing every few years; prefers more sun than shade
