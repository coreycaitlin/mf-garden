## Basic information
- **Scientific name:** Tiarella trifoliata
- **Plant type:** Perennial
- **USDA zones:** 4-8
- **Native region:** Pacific Northwest, from Alaska to California and Montana

## Growth characteristics
- **Mature height:** 12-20 inches (with flower stalks)
- **Mature spread:** 12-18 inches
- **Growth rate:** Medium
- **Lifespan:** Long-lived perennial

## Growing conditions
- **Sun requirements:** Part Shade/Full Shade
- **Water needs:** Medium
- **Soil type:** Rich, humusy, moist
- **Soil pH:** 5.5-7.0

## Seasonal interest
- **Bloom time:** May-July
- **Bloom color:** White to pale pink
- **Fall color:** Deciduous
- **Winter interest:** Dormant

## Wildlife value
- **Attracts:** Native bees, small pollinators
- **Host plant for:** N/A
- **Provides:** Nectar

## Care & maintenance
- **Pruning needs:** Remove spent flower stalks; clean up dead foliage in spring
- **Fertilizer:** Light compost in spring
- **Mulch:** 2 inches of leaf mulch
- **Special care:** Keep soil consistently moist

## Notes
- **Design notes:** Delicate, airy white flower spikes create a "foamy" effect; attractive maple-like leaves
- **Observations:**
- **Challenges:** Prefers consistent moisture but is more drought tolerant than often assumed
