# White Yarrow
![White Yarrow](../images/white-yarrow.jpg)
*Photo: [Rhododendrites](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Achillea_millefolium_(52354).jpg) | CC BY-SA 4.0*


## Basic information
- **Scientific name:** Achillea millefolium
- **Plant type:** Perennial
- **USDA zones:** 3-9
- **Native region:** Native across North America, Europe, and Asia (circumboreal)

## Growth characteristics
- **Mature height:** 1-3 feet
- **Mature spread:** 1-2 feet (spreads by rhizomes)
- **Growth rate:** Fast
- **Lifespan:** Long-lived perennial
- **Roots:**

## Growing conditions
- **Sun requirements:** Full Sun
- **Water needs:** Low (very drought tolerant)
- **Soil type:** Well-drained; tolerates poor, dry, sandy soils
- **Soil pH:** 4.0-8.0 (highly adaptable)
- **Native habitat:** Dry to moist open sites, rocky slopes, clearings

## Seasonal interest
- **Bloom time:** June-September
- **Bloom color:** White (native form); flat-topped clusters
- **Fall color:** Semi-evergreen ferny foliage
- **Winter interest:** Seed heads persist; basal foliage remains

## Wildlife value
- **Attracts:** Bees, butterflies, beneficial insects (ladybugs, lacewings, parasitic wasps)
- **Host plant for:** Painted lady butterfly
- **Provides:** Nectar, pollen; excellent insectary plant

- **Quantity needed:**
- **Location/bed:**
- **Spacing:** 12-18 inches apart
- **Companion plants:** Echinacea, black-eyed Susan, native grasses, asters

## Care & maintenance
- **Pruning needs:** Deadhead to prolong bloom and prevent excessive self-seeding; cut back in late fall
- **Fertilizer:** None needed; excess fertility causes floppy growth
- **Mulch:** Light or none; prefers lean conditions
- **Special care:** Very low maintenance; may need dividing every 3-4 years to control spread

## Notes
- **Design notes:** Ferny, aromatic foliage; flat flower clusters provide landing pads for butterflies; excellent meadow plant
- **Observations:**
- **Challenges:** Can spread aggressively in rich soil; choose native white form over colored cultivars for wildlife value
