# Cascade Penstemon
![Cascade Penstemon](../images/cascade-penstemon.jpg)
*Photo: [Mount Rainier National Park](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Cascade_Penstemon_(16834107040).jpg) | Public Domain*

## Basic information
- **Scientific name:** Penstemon serrulatus
- **Plant type:** Herbaceous Perennial
- **USDA zones:** 5-9
- **Native region:** Southern British Columbia to Oregon, both sides of the Cascades and Olympic Mountains

## Growth characteristics
- **Mature height:** 8-28 inches
- **Mature spread:** 12-18 inches
- **Growth rate:** Medium
- **Lifespan:** Short-lived perennial (3-5 years)

## Growing conditions
- **Sun requirements:** Full Sun/Part Shade
- **Water needs:** Medium (tolerates seasonally wet soils)
- **Soil type:** Moist, well-drained; tolerates various soil types
- **Soil pH:** 6.0-7.5

## Seasonal interest
- **Bloom time:** June-August
- **Bloom color:** Deep blue to purple
- **Fall color:** None significant
- **Winter interest:** Semi-evergreen basal foliage

## Wildlife value
- **Attracts:** Hummingbirds, native bees, butterflies, beneficial insects
- **Host plant for:** Various native moths and butterflies
- **Provides:** Nectar for pollinators throughout summer

- **Location/bed:**
- **Spacing:** 12-18 inches apart
- **Companion plants:** Other meadow perennials, grasses, seaside daisy

## Care & maintenance
- **Pruning needs:** Deadhead to extend bloom; cut back in late fall
- **Fertilizer:** None needed
- **Mulch:** Light mulch; avoid wet crown
- **Special care:** Best penstemon for wet Pacific Northwest winters; tolerates seasonally wet soils better than most penstemons

## Notes
- **Design notes:** One of the few penstemons native west of the Cascades; excellent for pollinator gardens, rain gardens, and rock gardens; less fussy than other penstemons
- **Observations:**
- **Challenges:** Short-lived but self-seeds readily
