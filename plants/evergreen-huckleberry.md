# Evergreen Huckleberry

## Basic information
- **Scientific name:** Vaccinium ovatum
- **Plant type:** Evergreen Shrub
- **USDA zones:** 7-9
- **Native region:** Pacific Coast from British Columbia to California

## Growth characteristics
- **Mature height:** 4-8 feet (up to 12 feet in shade)
- **Mature spread:** 4-6 feet
- **Growth rate:** Slow to medium
- **Lifespan:** Long-lived (decades)
- **Roots:**

## Growing conditions
- **Sun requirements:** Part Shade/Full Shade (tolerates full sun near coast)
- **Water needs:** Low-Medium (drought tolerant once established)
- **Soil type:** Acidic, well-drained, humus-rich
- **Soil pH:** 4.5-5.5 (acidic)
- **Native habitat:**

## Seasonal interest
- **Bloom time:** March-May
- **Bloom color:** White to pink, bell-shaped
- **Fall color:** Evergreen; new growth bronze-red
- **Winter interest:** Evergreen foliage; berries persist

## Wildlife value
- **Attracts:** Bees, birds, butterflies
- **Host plant for:** Various butterfly and moth species
- **Provides:** Berries for birds and mammals; excellent cover

- **Spacing:** 4-6 feet apart

## Care & maintenance
- **Pruning needs:** Minimal; prune to shape in late winter if desired
- **Fertilizer:** Acidic fertilizer if needed; usually not required in proper soil
- **Mulch:** 2-3 inches of acidic mulch (bark, needles)
- **Special care:** Protect from hot afternoon sun in inland areas

## Notes
- **Design notes:** Excellent hedge or screen plant; glossy dark green foliage prized by florists; edible berries sweeter than red huckleberry
- **Observations:**
- **Challenges:** Slow to establish; needs acidic soil
