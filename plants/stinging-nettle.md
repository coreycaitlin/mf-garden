- **Water needs:** Medium-High (prefers moist conditions)

## Seasonal interest
- **Bloom time:** June-September
- **Bloom color:** Greenish, inconspicuous
- **Fall color:** Dies back in fall
- **Winter interest:** None (herbaceous)

## Wildlife value
- **Attracts:** Butterflies (critical host plant)
- **Host plant for:** Red Admiral, Painted Lady, Milbert's Tortoiseshell, Satyr Comma butterflies
- **Provides:** Seeds for birds; exceptional butterfly habitat

## Care & maintenance
- **Pruning needs:** Cut back in fall or early spring; can cut multiple times for harvests
- **Fertilizer:** Generally not needed; thrives in nitrogen-rich soils
- **Mulch:** Light mulch; often self-mulches with leaf litter
- **Special care:** Wear gloves when handling; contain spread if needed with barriers

## Notes
- **Design notes:** Plant in a contained area or where spreading is acceptable; extremely valuable for butterfly habitat; young leaves edible when cooked (removes sting); traditional medicinal and fiber plant
- **Observations:**
- **Challenges:** Stinging hairs cause skin irritation; spreads aggressively
