# Douglas Iris
![Douglas Iris](../images/douglas-iris.jpg)

## Basic information
- **Scientific name:** Iris douglasiana
- **Plant type:** Perennial
- **USDA zones:** 7-9
- **Native region:** Pacific Coast, from Oregon to California

## Growth characteristics
- **Mature height:** 12-24 inches
- **Mature spread:** 12-18 inches (forms clumps)
- **Growth rate:** Medium
- **Lifespan:** Long-lived perennial

## Growing conditions
- **Sun requirements:** Full Sun/Part Shade
- **Water needs:** Low-Medium (drought tolerant once established)
- **Soil type:** Well-drained; tolerates clay and poor soils
- **Soil pH:** 5.5-7.0

## Seasonal interest
- **Bloom time:** March-May
- **Bloom color:** Blue-violet, lavender, cream, white (variable)
- **Fall color:** Evergreen grass-like foliage
- **Winter interest:** Evergreen foliage persists year-round

## Wildlife value
- **Attracts:** Native bees, bumblebees
- **Host plant for:** N/A
- **Provides:** Nectar; dense foliage provides cover

## Care & maintenance
- **Pruning needs:** Remove spent flower stalks; trim back tattered leaves in late winter
- **Fertilizer:** Generally not needed
- **Mulch:** Light mulch; avoid covering rhizomes
- **Special care:** Prefers dry conditions in summer (Mediterranean climate); avoid summer irrigation

## Notes
- **Design notes:** Elegant, iris blooms above grass-like evergreen foliage; excellent for borders, rock gardens, and naturalistic plantings; parent of many Pacific Coast hybrid iris
- **Observations:**
- **Challenges:** Can rot with excessive summer water; rhizomes should remain at soil surface
