# Wild Ginger

## Basic information
- **Scientific name:** Asarum caudatum
- **Plant type:** Perennial/Groundcover
- **USDA zones:** 4-8
- **Native region:** Pacific Northwest, from British Columbia to California

## Growth characteristics
- **Mature height:** 6 inches
- **Mature spread:** 12-24 inches (spreads by rhizomes)
- **Growth rate:** Slow to Medium
- **Lifespan:** Long-lived perennial

## Growing conditions
- **Sun requirements:** Part Shade/Full Shade
- **Water needs:** Medium
- **Soil type:** Rich, humusy, moist
- **Soil pH:** 5.0-7.0

## Seasonal interest
- **Bloom time:** April-July
- **Bloom color:** Brownish-purple (hidden under foliage)
- **Fall color:** Evergreen
- **Winter interest:** Yes - evergreen heart-shaped leaves

## Wildlife value
- **Attracts:** Ground beetles, native pollinators
- **Host plant for:** Pipevine swallowtail butterfly (occasional)
- **Provides:** Shelter for ground-dwelling insects

- **Quantity needed:**
- **Location/bed:**
- **Spacing:** 12 inches apart
- **Companion plants:** Sword fern, trillium, bleeding heart, inside-out flower

## Care & maintenance
- **Pruning needs:** None required
- **Fertilizer:** Light compost in spring if desired
- **Mulch:** Leaf litter (natural forest floor conditions)

- **Challenges:** Slow to establish; needs consistent moisture
