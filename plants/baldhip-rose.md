# Baldhip Rose

## Basic information
- **Scientific name:** Rosa gymnocarpa
- **Plant type:** Deciduous Shrub
- **USDA zones:** 5-9
- **Native region:** Pacific Northwest, from British Columbia to California

## Growth characteristics
- **Mature height:** 3-6 feet
- **Mature spread:** 3-5 feet
- **Growth rate:** Medium
- **Lifespan:** Long-lived shrub

## Growing conditions
- **Sun requirements:** Full Sun/Part Shade/Full Shade (tolerates wide range)
- **Water needs:** Low to High (adapts to dry through wet conditions)
- **Soil type:** Well-drained; tolerates poor, rocky soils
- **Soil pH:** 5.5-7.0

## Seasonal interest
- **Bloom time:** May-July
- **Bloom color:** Pink, small single flowers
- **Fall color:** Orange to red
- **Winter interest:** Red hips (without sepals - "baldhip"); reddish stems

## Wildlife value
- **Attracts:** Native bees, syrphid flies
- **Host plant for:** Western tussock moth, various other moths
- **Provides:** Rose hips for birds and mammals; cover and nesting

- **Location/bed:**
- **Spacing:** 4-5 feet apart
- **Companion plants:** Sword fern, salal, Oregon grape, snowberry

## Care & maintenance
- **Pruning needs:** Minimal; remove dead wood in late winter; can thin for shape
- **Fertilizer:** None needed
- **Mulch:** 2-3 inches of organic mulch
- **Special care:** Very low maintenance; one of the best roses for shade

## Notes
- **Design notes:** Delicate, graceful native rose; fine texture; tough species that handles wide range of conditions; named for hips that lack persistent sepals
- **Observations:**
- **Challenges:** Small thorns (less aggressive than other roses); may sucker slowly
