## Basic information
- **Scientific name:** Arctostaphylos manzanita 'Dr. Hurd'
- **Plant type:** Evergreen Shrub/Small Tree
- **USDA zones:** 7-10
- **Native region:** California (cultivar of species native to California chaparral)

## Growth characteristics
- **Mature height:** 12-16 feet
- **Mature spread:** 10-15 feet
- **Growth rate:** Medium-Fast
- **Lifespan:** Long-lived shrub (decades)

## Growing conditions
- **Sun requirements:** Full Sun/Part Shade
- **Water needs:** Low (drought tolerant once established)
- **Soil type:** Well-drained; tolerates clay, sandy, rocky, and poor soils
- **Soil pH:** 5.5-7.5

## Seasonal interest
- **Bloom time:** February-April
- **Bloom color:** White to pale pink
- **Fall color:** Evergreen
- **Winter interest:** Evergreen foliage; striking red-brown bark; berries

## Wildlife value
- **Attracts:** Native bees, hummingbirds, birds, butterflies
- **Host plant for:** Various native moths and butterflies
- **Provides:** Early-season nectar; berries for birds; year-round shelter

## Care & maintenance
- **Pruning needs:** Can prune in late summer to shape; can be trained as small tree
- **Fertilizer:** None needed; avoid fertilizing
- **Mulch:** Inorganic mulch or none; avoid organic mulch against trunk
- **Special care:** Prefers dry summers; do not overwater once established; more garden-tolerant than many manzanitas

## Notes
- **Design notes:** One of the largest manzanitas; beautiful smooth red-brown bark; can be pruned as multi-trunk small tree; excellent year-round structure; important early-season pollinator plant
- **Observations:**
- **Challenges:** Needs excellent drainage; can be sensitive to summer irrigation
