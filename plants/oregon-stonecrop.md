# Oregon Stonecrop
![Oregon Stonecrop](../images/oregon-stonecrop.jpg)
*Photo: [Walter Siegmund](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Sedum_oreganum_0958.JPG) | CC BY-SA 3.0*

## Basic information
- **Scientific name:** Sedum oreganum
- **Plant type:** Perennial / Groundcover / Succulent
- **USDA zones:** 5-9
- **Native region:** Pacific Northwest; British Columbia to northern California, west of the Cascades

## Growth characteristics
- **Mature height:** 2-6 inches
- **Mature spread:** 6-12 inches (spreads to form mats)
- **Growth rate:** Slow to Medium
- **Lifespan:** Long-lived perennial
- **Roots:** Shallow, fibrous

## Growing conditions
- **Sun requirements:** Full Sun/Part Shade
- **Water needs:** Low (very drought tolerant)
- **Soil type:** Well-drained, rocky, sandy; tolerates poor soils
- **Soil pH:** 5.5-7.5
- **Native habitat:** Rocky outcrops, cliffs, rocky slopes, talus

## Seasonal interest
- **Bloom time:** June-August
- **Bloom color:** Yellow
- **Fall color:** Evergreen; may turn reddish in cold/dry conditions
- **Winter interest:** Evergreen succulent foliage

## Wildlife value
- **Attracts:** Native bees, butterflies
- **Host plant for:** Various native insects
- **Provides:** Nectar; groundcover habitat

- **Location/bed:**
- **Spacing:** 6-8 inches apart
- **Companion plants:** Other sedums, penstemons, fescues, yarrow

## Care & maintenance
- **Pruning needs:** None required
- **Fertilizer:** None needed; thrives in lean soil
- **Mulch:** Gravel mulch if any; avoid organic mulch near crown
- **Special care:** Excellent drainage essential; very low maintenance

## Notes
- **Design notes:** Excellent for rock gardens, green roofs, rock walls, and dry areas; fleshy succulent leaves; spreads to form attractive mats
- **Observations:**
- **Challenges:** Needs excellent drainage; may rot in wet winter soils
