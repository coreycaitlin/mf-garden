## Basic information
- **Scientific name:** Dicentra formosa
- **Plant type:** Perennial
- **USDA zones:** 4-8
- **Native region:** Pacific Northwest, from British Columbia to California

## Growth characteristics
- **Mature height:** 12-18 inches
- **Mature spread:** 12-18 inches
- **Growth rate:** Medium
- **Lifespan:** Long-lived perennial

## Growing conditions
- **Sun requirements:** Part Shade/Full Shade
- **Water needs:** Medium
- **Soil type:** Rich, moist, well-drained
- **Soil pH:** 5.5-7.0

## Seasonal interest
- **Bloom time:** March-July (long bloom season)
- **Bloom color:** Pink to rose-purple
- **Fall color:** Foliage dies back
- **Winter interest:** Dormant

## Wildlife value
- **Attracts:** Hummingbirds, native bees
- **Host plant for:** Clodius Parnassian butterfly
- **Provides:** Nectar

## Care & maintenance
- **Pruning needs:** Cut back dead foliage in late fall or early spring
- **Fertilizer:** Light compost application in spring
- **Mulch:** 2 inches of leaf mulch
- **Special care:** May go dormant in dry summer conditions

## Notes
- **Design notes:** Delicate, fern-like foliage with heart-shaped flowers; naturalizes well
- **Observations:**
- **Challenges:** Will self-seed; can spread via rhizomes
