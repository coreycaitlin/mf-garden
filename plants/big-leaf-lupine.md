# Big Leaf Lupine

## Basic information
- **Scientific name:** Lupinus polyphyllus
- **Plant type:** Perennial
- **USDA zones:** 4-8
- **Native region:** Pacific Northwest, from Alaska to California

## Growth characteristics
- **Mature height:** 3-5 feet
- **Mature spread:** 2-3 feet
- **Growth rate:** Fast
- **Lifespan:** Short-lived perennial (3-5 years, self-seeds)

## Growing conditions
- **Sun requirements:** Full Sun/Part Sun
- **Water needs:** Medium
- **Soil type:** Moist, well-drained; fixes nitrogen
- **Soil pH:** 5.5-7.0 (prefers slightly acidic)

## Seasonal interest
- **Bloom time:** May-July
- **Bloom color:** Blue, purple, sometimes pink or white
- **Fall color:** Foliage dies back
- **Winter interest:** Dormant

## Wildlife value
- **Attracts:** Bumblebees, native bees, butterflies
- **Host plant for:** Silvery blue, Boisduval's blue, and other Lycaenid butterflies
- **Provides:** Nectar, pollen; nitrogen fixation improves soil

## Care & maintenance
- **Pruning needs:** Deadhead spent flower spikes to encourage reblooming; cut back in fall
- **Fertilizer:** None needed (fixes nitrogen)
- **Mulch:** Light mulch
- **Special care:** Do not disturb taproot; best planted from seed or small transplants

## Notes
- **Design notes:** Bold vertical spikes make dramatic statement; palmate leaves add texture; parent of many garden lupine hybrids
- **Observations:**
- **Challenges:** Short-lived but self-seeds; may need staking; can get aphids; all parts toxic if ingested
