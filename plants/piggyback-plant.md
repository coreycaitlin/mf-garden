# Piggyback Plant

## Basic information
- **Scientific name:** Tolmiea menziesii
- **Plant type:** Perennial/Groundcover
- **USDA zones:** 6-9
- **Native region:** Pacific Northwest, from Alaska to California

## Growth characteristics
- **Mature height:** 12-24 inches
- **Mature spread:** 12-24 inches
- **Growth rate:** Fast
- **Lifespan:** Long-lived perennial

## Growing conditions
- **Sun requirements:** Part Shade/Full Shade
- **Water needs:** Low-Medium (more drought tolerant than expected)
- **Soil type:** Rich, moist, well-drained
- **Soil pH:** 5.5-7.0

## Seasonal interest
- **Bloom time:** April-June
- **Bloom color:** Maroon
- **Fall color:** Semi-evergreen
- **Winter interest:** Foliage persists in mild winters

## Wildlife value
- **Attracts:** Small flies (pollinators)
- **Host plant for:** N/A
- **Provides:** Groundcover shelter

## Care & maintenance
- **Pruning needs:** Remove tired leaves as needed
- **Fertilizer:** Light compost in spring
- **Mulch:** 2 inches of organic mulch
- **Special care:** Plantlets form on leaves and root where they touch ground; more drought tolerant than often assumed

## Notes
- **Design notes:** Unique reproduction - baby plants grow on mother leaves ("piggyback"); textured maple-like foliage
- **Observations:**
- **Challenges:** Can look ragged in very dry conditions
